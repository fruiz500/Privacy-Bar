Password Privacy Help

How Password Synthesis Works
Privacy Bar does not "store" your passwords in the traditional sense. Instead, it synthesizes a unique, strong password for every website based on your Master Key (MK) and the website's domain name.
  • Zero-Knowledge: Your MK is never saved or sent outside the app.
  • Portable Settings: Only non-secret items (password length, allowed characters, and the "Serial" code) are saved to your Chrome profile so they are available on all your devices.
The Master Key (MK) & Hashili
Your security depends entirely on a strong MK. Use a long, unpredictable passphrase.
  • Strength Bar: Located below the MK field, this analyzes your password as you type. If your MK is weak, the app adds extra computations to compensate.
  • The Hashili Word: When you stop typing for one second, a mnemonic "hashili" word appears. Since you cannot see your MK, this word confirms you typed it correctly. If the hashili is different than usual, you made a typo.
  • Visibility: Click the eye icon to reveal your MK if needed.
  • Warning: If you forget your MK, it cannot be recovered. There are no hints or reminders.
How to Synthesize and Fill a Password
  1. Ensure the Password tab is open (it should open automatically if a password field is detected).
  2. Enter your MK correctly (check the hashili).
  3. Click Synthesize and Fill.
  4. The password will be injected directly into the webpage. If injection fails, it will be copied to your clipboard automatically.
How to Use or Store Custom Passwords PRO
If a website requires a specific password that isn't synthesized:
  1. Ensure your MK is filled.
  2. Click Use/Change Stored.
  3. First time: A popup will ask you to enter the password. It is stored encrypted by your MK, so Google never sees the actual text.
  4. Subsequent uses: Clicking the button will decrypt and fill the password after you confirm in a popup.
  5. Delete/Change: Click Cancel when the confirmation popup appears to delete or modify the stored entry.
Note: This feature requires a Pro license.
How to Change a Password
To update a password while still using a synthesized one:
  1. Navigate to the site's "Change Password" page.
  2. Click Change Password. The old password is re-synthesized and copied to your clipboard.
  3. Change a parameter, such as the Serial number, to generate a new password.
  4. Click Synthesize and Fill to put the new password in the "New Password" fields.
  5. Paste the old password from your clipboard into the "Old Password" field.
Special Parameters (Length, Characters, Serial)
You can tailor passwords to meet site-specific requirements:
  • Length: Enter a whole number (default is 43).
  • Allowed Characters: Use keywords like alpha (all letters), uppercase, lowercase, numbers or numeric, alphanumeric (both letters and numbers), or hex. You can also type a specific set of allowed symbols.
  • Serial: Change this value to generate a different password for the same site without changing your MK.
Using Privacy Bar on Mobile

While most mobile browsers do not currently support Chrome extensions, you can still access your synthesized passwords and encrypt/decrypt messages on any device (basic modes only) thanks to the deterministic nature of our math.

Privacy Bar Mobile:
https://privacybar.net/mobile
  • Parity: Because the generation is mathematical, entering the same Master Key, Host, and Serial on your phone will produce the exact same password as the desktop extension.
  • Security: No data is sent to a server; all processing occurs locally in your mobile browser's memory.
  • Pro Tip: Open the link in Safari (iOS) or Chrome (Android) and use the "Add to Home Screen" option to use it like a standalone app.
